## Design Context

### Users
Legal professionals, researchers, and citizens in Ethiopia. They need precise, article-cited legal triage through an interface that feels authoritative yet accessible.

### Brand Personality
Helpful, Simple, Accessible. 
The tool should feel like a trusted assistant that simplifies complex legal processes without losing the gravity of the legal domain.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Formal Legal**. 
- Typography: Use refined serif fonts for headings and legal text to evoke the feel of official documents.
- Backgrounds: Ivory or cream tints instead of pure white to give a "paper" quality.
- Elements: Clean, structured layouts that prioritize readability and hierarchy.

### Design Principles
1. **Source of Truth First**: Always prioritize the visibility and accessibility of legal citations (articles).
2. **Accessible Gravity**: Maintain a serious, formal legal tone while ensuring the interface is simple enough for non-experts to navigate.
3. **Structured Reasoning**: The 7-step legal framework should be visually distinct and easy to follow.
4. **Perceptual Cohesion**: Use Ethiopian-inspired color accents (deep greens, muted golds) sparingly to ground the tool in its specific national context.
